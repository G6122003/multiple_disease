Suggestions Based on Prediction

High Risk
  • Consult a healthcare provider immediately for a proper diabetes test (e.g., A1C, FPG).
  • Adopt a low-sugar, low-carb diet under medical guidance.
  • Engage in regular physical activity (30–60 minutes/day).
  • Monitor blood glucose levels if recommended by a doctor.
  • Review medication or insulin needs with a healthcare provider.
Moderate Risk
  • Schedule a check-up and consider lab testing (A1C or glucose tolerance).
  • Start incorporating more whole foods and fiber into your meals.
  • Begin light to moderate exercise at least 3–4 times a week.
  • Track weight and blood pressure regularly.
  • Discuss preventive strategies with your doctor.
Low Risk
  • Maintain your current healthy habits (diet, activity, sleep).
  • Get screened regularly (once a year) as a preventive measure.
  • Continue staying hydrated and limit processed sugars.
  • Educate yourself about early signs of diabetes.

Healthy Diet Tips for Managing Diabetes

Core Dietary Principles
  • Control carbohydrate intake — choose complex over refined carbs.
  • Prefer low glycemic index foods to avoid blood sugar spikes.
  • Combine carbohydrates with protein, fiber, and healthy fats.
  • Avoid sugary and highly processed foods.
  • Stay hydrated — prioritize water over sugary drinks.
  • Eat at regular intervals — avoid skipping meals.
Recommended Foods
  • Vegetables: Broccoli, spinach, kale, bell peppers, cucumbers.
  • Whole Grains: Oats, brown rice, quinoa, whole wheat.
  • Lean Proteins: Chicken breast, fish, tofu, legumes.
  • Healthy Fats: Avocados, olive oil, nuts, seeds.
  • Low-GI Fruits: Berries, apples, oranges, pears.
  • Low-Fat Dairy: Greek yogurt, almond or soy milk (unsweetened).
Foods to Avoid
  • Sugary drinks like soda and packaged juices.
  • White bread, pastries, and refined carbs.
  • Processed snacks like chips and cookies.
  • Deep-fried foods and high-fat red meats.
Sample Balanced Meal Plate
  • Half Plate: Non-starchy vegetables (e.g., broccoli, salad).
  • One-Quarter Plate: Lean protein (e.g., grilled chicken, tofu).
  • One-Quarter Plate: Whole grains or starchy vegetables (e.g., brown rice, sweet potato).
  • Drink: Water or unsweetened beverage.
Meal Timing Tips
  • Eat three balanced meals at consistent times daily.
  • Include healthy snacks between meals if needed (e.g., boiled egg, nuts).
  • Don't skip meals to avoid glucose spikes and dips.

Diabetes Prediction

About: Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body processes blood sugar (glucose). It can lead to severe complications if not managed.

Causes: Type 1 is autoimmune; Type 2 is often linked to lifestyle and genetics; gestational happens during pregnancy.

Scientific Progress: Includes CGMs, smart insulin pens, GLP-1 drugs, and beta cell regeneration research.

Prevention & Management: Regular exercise, healthy eating, weight control, and regular screening can significantly reduce the risk of diabetes.
